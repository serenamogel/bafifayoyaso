Who is Anneliese Judge?

American actress Anneliese Judge was born under the zodiac sign Sagittarius on 17 December 2001, in Pinehurst, North Carolina USA. She has only two acting credits, and is perhaps known best for starring as Annie Sullivan in all 30 episodes of the romantic series “Sweet Magnolias”, which was created by Sheryl J. Anderson in 2020 and today also stars Heather Headley, Brooke Elliott and JoAnna Garcia Swisher. The series follows the lives of three women who’ve been best friends since high school, and it’s won two of its three award nominations.

Anneliese’s currently shooting for the upcoming movie “Theo”.

Education and early life

Anneliese was raised in Pinehurst alongside her younger brother William, by their parents about whom Anneliese prefers not to speak in public because she respects their privacy, but whom she’s still featured in a couple of pictures and videos which she’s posted onto her social media accounts; her brother’s recently earned his black belt in Taekwondo.

Anneliese studied at Pinecrest High School and was into multiple activities during her time there as she enjoyed dancing, appearing in school plays and running track. She was 16 when noticed by a modelling scout who helped her sign her first contract, and this eventually led to Anneliese’s transition to acting.

She matriculated in 2020 and continued her education at the University of Carolina, Greensboro from which she’s set to graduate with a Bachelor’s degree in marketing and international business in 2024.

Acting and other credits

Anneliese has only one other acting credit, playing Jessica Waters in the 2021 horror thriller movie “Where’s Rose”, which starred Cara AnnMarie and Ty Simpkins, and was written and directed by John Mathis. It follows a girl who’s gone missing and has now been found, while only her brother’s been able to discover that an evil force has returned with her; the film was nominated for three awards.

In 2022, Anneliese made a guest appearance in talk-shows “Popternative” and “In Creative Company”.
