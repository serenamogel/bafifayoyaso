Income Tax Conviction

Wesley Snipes was charged with conspiring to cheat the US Government and another count of making a false claim for payment against the government in 2006, barely two years after the release of Blade Trilogy.

In addition, he was also accused with six more charges of not filing tax forms.

Wesley was accused of failing to file tax returns between 1999 and 2004.

Snipes earned $37.9 million from his acting career between 1996 and 2004 and allegedly did not pay any taxes.

Wesley Snipes was acquitted of all felony counts in 2008, but he was convicted of three minor charges of neglecting to file tax returns.

As a result, the actor received a three-year prison sentence before being freed in 2013. He was placed under home arrest for the remainder of his sentence.

Wesley was also ordered to pay the IRS $17 million in overdue taxes, interest, and penalties in addition to his prison sentence.

Wesley Snipes | Career

Wesley Snipes appeared in a few television shows before being discovered by a casting agent. In 1986, he got his first role in the film “Wildcats.”

In 1987, Wesley played the role of a gang leader in Michael Jackson’s song video “Bad.”

After appearing in the music video, he got important roles in Hollywood, beginning in 1989 with “Major League.”

In 1991, he acted in the drama picture “Jungle Fever.” The film received a lot of positive feedback from critics and was nominated for several top awards.

Similarly, he was cast as the main character in “Blade” movie. Released in 1998, the film grossed over $150 million worldwide and became Snipes’ biggest commercial hit.

In addition, Snipes returned to the role of Blade in “Blade: Trinity,” which he also produced in 2004.

Furthermore, in 2017, Wesley Snipes joined the cast of “The Recall.”
