Puff Daddy: Charity

The Grammy-Award winner is also known for his philanthropic effort and mainly supports children, education, and youths.

In 1995, Combs launched Daddy’s House Social Programs, an organization dedicated to assisting inner-city youngsters.

In the 2003 New York Marathon, he raised more than $2 million for New York schools and children with HIV and AIDS.

He also offered $1 million to help victims of Hurricane Katrina in 2005, alongside Jay-Z and donated apparel from his Sean John line to the victims.

Besides that, the rapper has given laptops to schools in New York.

Combs was honored by the American Academy of Achievement with the Golden Plate Award in 1998.

In honor of Combs’ charitable endeavors, Chicago Mayor Richard M. Daley declared October 13, 2006, “Diddy Day.”

The star also contributed $1 million to Howard University in 2016 to establish the Sean Combs Scholarship Fund, which aids students who cannot afford their tuition.

Puff Daddy: Career

During his career beginnings, Combs worked as an intern at New York’s Uptown Records.

No Way Out, his debut album, was released on July 22, 1997, through Bad Boy Records.

Beyond leaving his mark on the music industry, Combs is an influential sartorial force.

He founded Sean John, a clothing line, in 1998. This was an enormous and immediate commercial success.

Sean John was sold to Global Brands Group in 2016.

Apart from his music career, Sean Combs has also enjoyed a successful acting career. He made his first movie appearance in 2001 Made.

In 2002, Fortune magazine ranked Combs 12th among the top 40 entrepreneurs under 40.

In 2013, he founded Combs Enterprises.

According to Forbes, Daddy ranked No. 1 on 2013’s list of hip-hop’s wealthiest artists, with an estimated net worth of $580 million.
