It was disheartening when The Fosters fans learned after the season two finale that one of the drama’s most popular characters — Jesus, played by Jake T. Austin — wasn’t recast. The drama’s second season ended on a cliffhanger that came as a shock to many.

In the final episode of the second season, Ana was seen confused about who to give her unborn child to for adoption – her parents or the couple (Stef and Lena) who adopted her other two children, Jesus and Mariana, but before they decide she went into labor and on the way to In the Hospital with Jesus and Mariana they had an accident that resulted in death.

Still in shock and awaiting the next finding out who died in the accident, fans were greeted with Jake T Austin’s tweets announcing his departure from the show. This revelation, of course, answered the question of who died in the car crash, but left many wondering why Jake T. Austin actually left the show. Here are all the facts and details you’ve been looking for for years

Brief Bio – Ethnicity

The former child actor who has graced the big screen since ten was born on December 3, 1994 in New York to Joe Szymanski and Giny Rodriquez Toranzo. His full name is Jake Austin Szymanski. The “T” in his stage name Jake T Austin was derived from his mother’s maiden name “Toranzo”.

He is multi-ethnic. His father is of Polish, Irish and English descent and his mother is of Argentine, Spanish and Puerto Rican descent. Jake is proud of his multicultural heritage and embraces it, although he embraces his Portorican heritage all the more. Jake is not his parents’ only child, he has a younger sister named Ava.

career

His acting career took off in 2003 with his first recorded acting role as Kid 1698 in a comedy sketch The Late Show Starring David Letterman, which came after he appeared in a television commercial the previous year. In 2004, he landed his first starring role as the voice of Dora’s cousin Diego in the Nickelodeon animated series Dora the Explorer, and reprized the character in another animated series Go Diego Go! . Jake also lent his voice to The Ant Bully and Everybody’s Hero.

He played Angel Macias in The Perfect Game (2006), which was released more than three years later due to post-production financial constraints. In 2007, Jake T Austin had a role in a Walt Disney film Johnny Kapahala: Back on Board as Chris. That same year, he landed another role as Max Russo in another Disney film , Wizards of Waverly Place , which remains one of his most popular works to this day.

The former child actor would later reprise the role in a couple of other Disney spin-offs, the Wizards on Deck crossover episode, which also included The Suite Life on Deck and Hannah Montana.

In 2013, he began playing the role of Jesus Foster on an ABC family drama series The Fosters, appearing in 41 episodes before the end of the second season. His other films and TV series include Rio I & II, Drop Dead Diva, Law & Order: Special Victims Unit, Justice League vs. Teen Titans, Teen Titans: The Judas Treaty, Justice League Action, Formerly The Emoji Movie among others .

Jake appeared on Latina magazine’s list of Brightest Stars Under 25. He once presented an award at the Teen’s Choice Awards.

Why did Jake T Austin leave the Fosters?

Now back to why Jake T. Austin left the Fosters. After announcing his departure from the show at the end of season two, there was a rumor that he had quit because he didn’t want to play a teen role anymore.

Is he gay or does he have a girlfriend?

Jake T Austin isn’t gay, at least he didn’t come out as one. In 2016, the Wizards of Waverly Place star revealed in an interview with the Huffington Post that he was dating one of his fans named Danielle Ceasar.

Their love story and how they met has a little twist. Jake first met Danielle in 2012 at Planet Hollywood in New York City. After five years of tweeting about Ceasar frequently, following Instagram comments, she asked for a photo with him, which he agreed to. After meeting he started following her on Twitter, one thing led to another and here they are together today.
