Ben Finegold (born September 6, 1969 in Detroit, Michigan) is a chess Grandmaster who holds the title of “Master of the World.” Finegold was born into a chess family and achieved the rank of USCF Master at the age of 14 years old. He earned a Life Master at the age of 15, a Senior Master at the age of 16, and an International Master at the age of 20. Finegold tied for first place in the U.S. Open Chess Championships in 1994 (Chicago, IL) and 2007 (Cherry Hill, NJ), respectively. During the 2002 World Open (Philadelphia, PA), he tied for first place (and earned a Grandmaster norm in the process), and he also tied for first place in the 2005 and 2008 National Chess Championships (Las Vegas, NV). According to the United States Chess Federation’s August 2013 rating list, he is one of the top 40 players in the country.  Finegold has competed in nine U.S. Chess Championships: in 1994 in Key West, FL, in 1999 in Salt Lake City, UT, in 2002 in Seattle, WA, in 2005 in La Jolla, CA, in 2006 in San Diego, CA, in 2008 in Tulsa, OK, in 2010 in Saint Louis, MO, in 2011 in Saint Louis, MO, and in 2013 in Saint Louis, MO (Saint Louis, MO). In September 2009, he was awarded the rank of Grandmaster. Prior to his retirement on August 14, 2012, he served as Grandmaster-in-Residence at the Chess Club and Scholastic Center of Saint Louis. At the United States Chess Championship, the United States Junior Chess Championship, and the Sinquefield Cup, he provides live commentary.
