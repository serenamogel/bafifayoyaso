Carlos Alcaraz Stats and Career

Carlos Alcaraz started playing tennis at the age of four and showed great talent and potential from an early age.

He was ranked as high as world No. 22 as a junior and won two titles on the ITF Junior Circuit.

He turned professional in 2018 and won three titles on the ITF Men’s World Tennis Tour and four on the ATP Challenger Tour.

broke into the top 100 in rankings in May 2021.

Carlos Alcaraz wins

In July 2021, he reached his first ATP Tour final at the Croatia Open, an ATP 250 tournament, where he won his first title by defeating Richard Gasquet in straight sets.

He later entered the top 50 after reaching the quarterfinals at the US Open where he defeated Stefanos Tsitsipas in five sets before losing to Felix Auger-Aliassime.

In February 2022, he won his first ATP 500 title at the Rio Open by defeating Cristian Garin in three sets.

He then won his first Masters 1000 title at the Miami Open by defeating Jannik Sinner in three sets.

He also won his second ATP 500 title at the Barcelona Open by defeating Stefanos Tsitsipas in two sets.

In September 2022, he won his first Grand Slam title at the US Open by defeating Casper Ruud in four sets in the final.

He became the youngest man to win a Grand Slam title since

Rafael Nadal in 2005 and the youngest US Open champion since Pete Sampras in 1990.

In May 2023, he was awarded the Laureus World Breakthrough of the Year award for his remarkable achievements in tennis.

In June 2023, he won his first title on grass at the Queen’s Club Championship by defeating Alex de Minaur in straight sets.
