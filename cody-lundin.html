Cody Lundin Biography

Cody Lundin is the sole child in his family. He was the son of a military father. He did spend the majority of his childhood moving. He finally settled in Laramie, Wyoming. He completed his junior and high school studies in this location. He did live on the streets for a while after finishing high school.

He enrolled in a college in Prescott, Arizona. Prescott College awarded him a degree in Depth Psychology and Holistic Health. After some time on the streets, he moved to the backyards of friends, and later, he would live in a brush shelter.

Career

Career Lundin has been able to write two books that focus on Survival and preparedness. The titles of the two books are 98.6 Degrees: The Art of Keeping Your Ass Alive and When All Hell Breaks Loose: Stuff You Need to Survive When Disaster Strikes.

In 2014, he hosted a show called ‘Lost in the Wild,’ which aired on the Discovery Channel. He has also had the opportunity to host a television show called ‘Dual Survival.’ From 2010 to 2014, this show was on the air.

During the show, he demonstrated considerable survival skills while wearing shorts in all weather and without shoes. In February 2014, he informed his fans that he had left the television shows because he disagreed with the producers on safety issues.

He is currently residing in Northern Arizona. His home does not have electricity, so he relies on solar power. He usually collects rainwater because there is no running water. He does not pay for the services he receives. He is currently the owner of Survival Skills School. The school’s name is the Aboriginal Living Skills School. He does teach various survival techniques at this school.
